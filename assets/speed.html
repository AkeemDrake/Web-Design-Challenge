City vs Wind Speed

The graph does seem to show some sort of correlation. It seems to have three different types of cities. The cities that are on the two extremes, high latitude and low latitude, seem have have the higher overall wind speeds. The cities that are closer to the equator, however, seem to have a lower overall wind speed. Wind is caused by a change in atmospheric pressure and the rotation of the earth. It makes sense that the cities with high and low altitudes have higher wind speeds since they are farther away than the most neutral place: the equator.
